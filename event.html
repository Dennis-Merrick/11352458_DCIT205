2023 Annual Plants and Flower Fair

Date:
Friday, December 1, 2023 - 08:30
Venue:
University of Ghana Main Football Field

The Centre for Climate Change and Sustainability Studies is delighted to extend an invitation to the university community (faculty, staff, and students) for the upcoming 2023 Annual Plants and Flower Fair. This year's fair is under the theme “Change the Way We Stay Alive: Embracing Plants for Health and Earth Well-Being. " It is scheduled to take place on December 1–3, 2023, at the University of Ghana Main Football Field (Opposite University Basic School, Legon)

Why Attend the Fair?
This year’s fair is not just a celebration of the intrinsic beauty of plants and flowers but a clarion call to recognize their profound impact on our health and the planet’s sustainability. Amidst our busy lives, we often overlook the sheer power of nature to transform our well-being and to offer solutions to pressing environmental challenges. The fair aims to illuminate the myriad benefits of plants—from air purification and stress reduction to their role in a health-promoting plant-based diet.

What to Expect?
Prepare to be immersed in a vibrant showcase of floricultural exhibits, gain practical knowledge from medicinal plant gardens, and engage with expert talks that unravel the nexus between human health and botany. Interactive gardening workshops and environmental awareness campaigns will provide hands-on experience and knowledge to spark a green transformation in our daily lives.

Join the Green Movement
This event is a prime opportunity for students, faculty, and staff to contribute to and learn about sustainable practices that promote a healthier lifestyle and a thriving Earth. By participating, you’re not just attending a fair, but you're becoming part of a growing community dedicated to making positive changes for our health and environment.

We encourage all to come and discover how a closer connection with plants can enrich your life and assist in sculpting a sustainable future for all. Your presence is vital to the success of this communal educational journey.

Distribution of Laptops - One Student One Laptop (1S1L) initiative

Date:
Wednesday, November 8, 2023 - 09:00
Venue:
Cedi Conference Centre

The Vice Chancellor’s office at the University of Ghana is thrilled to bring to your attention the "One Student One Laptop" (1S1L) initiative which commenced in 2021. This visionary program aims to provide each student with a laptop, a crucial tool for both academic and personal growth.

In today's digital age, technology access is not a luxury but a necessity. The Vice-Chancellor believes that every student should have the opportunity to excel academically, enhance their skills, and prepare for the challenges of the modern world.

Therefore, the 1S1L initiative seeks to address the lack of technology skills caused by the inaccessibility of learning systems and tools that a functional laptop can offer.

To ensure equal access to technology, a select number of laptops will be provided entirely free of charge based on academic performance, financial need, and potential contributions to our academic community. This free laptop assistance is facilitated through the Students Financial Aid Office, eliminating financial barriers to students’ success.

Our inaugural 1S1L distribution took place in August 2022, and we've successfully conducted two more since then.

We're excited to announce the next distribution on November 8, 2023, at 9:00 a.m., which will be held at the Cedi Conference Center. We warmly extend an invitation to everyone to join us for this event.

We also wish to extend our heartfelt appreciation to our dedicated individual and corporate sponsors whose generous support in donating laptops to students has been invaluable, and upon whom we will continue to rely for our 1S1L initiative.’

For those who may not qualify for a free laptop but still need affordable access, we are offering hire purchase options. These laptops will be available to you at a significantly reduced cost, payable in convenient instalments throughout your school year.

In addition to providing laptops, we are establishing an assembling plant on our campus. This facility will not only contribute to student job creation, economic development, and project sustainability, but it will also provide students with a unique opportunity to gain hands-on experience in technology assembly, contributing to skills development and future employability.

This initiative is a testament to the Vice Chancellor’s commitment to student growth and success. The VC believes that access to technology is a fundamental right, and this program is a significant step toward creating a level playing field for all our students.

Additional information on the application and selection process is available on the University’s website.

UGSoL 2023 Alumni Homecoming Event

Date:
Monday, October 30, 2023 - 13:00 to Sunday, November 5, 2023 - 13:00
Venue:
University of Ghana, Legon

The University of Ghana School of Law (UGSoL) wishes to extend its warm invitation to you for the 2023 Alumni Homecoming. This year's Homecoming carries the theme, “Investing in Excellence, Empowering the Future of Legal Education in Ghana.” It promises to be a remarkable event, reuniting former students, celebrating achievements, and envisioning a brighter future for legal education in Ghana.

The week-long event is scheduled to take place from Monday, 30th October to Sunday, 5th November 2023.

The School's Alumni Homecoming is a cherished tradition that aims to reconnect with our alumni, faculty, and students from various generations. It provides a platform for networking, reminiscing, and rekindling old friendships. It is a time to reflect on the incredible journey of our students since their time at the School and to celebrate their accomplishments in the legal field and beyond.

This year's Homecoming holds special significance as we delve into the critical topic of legal education in Ghana. The legal landscape is evolving rapidly, and the need to invest in excellence and empower the future generation of legal practitioners has never been more apparent. The School has assembled a distinguished panel of speakers and experts who will shed light on the challenges and opportunities facing legal education and discuss the pivotal role our alumni can play in shaping its future.

For those unable to attend in person, we will provide virtual access to selected Homecoming events via Zoom, YouTube, and Facebook. Details for virtual participation will be available on the UGSoL's website.

We eagerly anticipate welcoming you back Home!
